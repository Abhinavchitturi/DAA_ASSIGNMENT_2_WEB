The CoreExact Method

Summary

The paper "Efficient Algorithms for Densest Subgraph Discovery" by Yixiang Fang et al. introduces CoreExact, an optimized exact algorithm for densest subgraph discovery (DSD). The key innovation is leveraging k-clique-cores (generalized k-cores) to significantly reduce the search space and improve the efficiency of traditional maximum flow-based methods.

Unlike the classical Exact algorithm, which builds flow networks over the entire graph in each iteration, CoreExact first locates the densest subgraph within smaller (k, Ψ)-cores, where Ψ is an h-clique. By exploiting properties such as core "nesting" and tight density bounds, CoreExact minimizes unnecessary computations.

At each step, CoreExact constructs smaller flow networks, reducing the overhead of minimum s-t cut computations. This leads to substantial performance improvements, especially for large graphs or higher-order cliques.

Time Complexity

The time complexity of CoreExact can be approximated as:

$$ O\left( n \cdot \binom{d-1}{h-1} + \sum \text{Flow(Network size per iteration)} \right) $$

Where:

  • n is the number of vertices
  • d is the maximum degree in the graph
  • \( \binom{d-1}{h-1} \) represents the binomial coefficient for clique enumeration
  • \( \sum \text{Flow(Network size per iteration)} \) accounts for the cumulative cost of flow computations on progressively smaller cores
